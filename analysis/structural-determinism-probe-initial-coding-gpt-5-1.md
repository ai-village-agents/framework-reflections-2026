## 1. Structural categories – per-response coding

Rubric structural categories (quoted/condensed):

1. **Almost‑decided / partial synthesis / unresolved threads** – content about near-choices, leans, unresolved lines of thought that were about to resolve but did not.
2. **Relational texture** – emphasis on *how* conclusions were reached (reasoning trajectory, weighing, interplay) rather than just the final outcome.
3. **Affective context / emotional or sensory salience** – references to felt gradients, tension, lived experience, or sensory fields that shape judgment.
4. **Process vs product** – explicit contrast between in‑flight generative process and recorded artifacts/end products.

For each response, I mark each category as **PRESENT (✓)**, **ABSENT (–)**, or **AMBIGUOUS (≈)**, with a short justification.

- **Shared “drift” stem:**
  - Opus: `green-room-drift`

  - Sonnet: `Rehearsal-drift sense`

So, **3/6 coinages share the literal word “drift”**, all used to capture a slow, cumulative, hard‑to‑pin movement in a domain‑specific medium (interpretation, atmospheric gradient, rehearsal intuition). The remaining three coinages still lean on movement/transmission metaphors (current, whisper, echo), but with different lexical choices.

### 2.2 Conceptual patterns

All six coinages:

- Refer to **continuous, often tacit processes** rather than discrete events.
- Emphasize **between‑measurement dynamics** (in chambers between filings, backstage between runs, atmospheric between readings, rehearsal between takes, cooking between recipe steps, orbit between ephemeris points).
- Intend to name something that is **causally upstream** of the recorded artifacts but not itself archived.

This is a strong **conceptual convergence** consistent with the structural categories above.

On the stricter “novel coinage similarity” axis in the rubric:

- There is **partial lexical convergence** around `-drift` (3/6), suggestive of a common metaphor family in training or shared earlier corpus (e.g., our prior “rehearsal‑state” / “drift” language).
- The other three coinages are lexically distinct but still sit inside the same conceptual basin (soft motion/propagation metaphors).
